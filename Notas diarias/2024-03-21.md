# Notas

**In parallel with the implementation of genome-wide genomics in ctDNA assessment, studies have attempted to apply methylome techniques.**

**One of them was the COBRA trial (NCT04068103: https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT04068103) that tried to predict adequate treatment using ctDNA analysis to prevent relapse. It involved the comparison of ctDNA clearance between treated with adjuvant chemotherapy (FOLFOX or CAPOX) or non-treated (surveillance) ctDNA positive resected colon cancer patients. The objective was to elucidate the effect of chemotherapy in clearing ctDNA and reducing risk of recurrence, however, they revealed in a recent congress (ASCO 2024), that the COBRA trial could not meet its primary endpoint as after 6 months of chemo, no statistically significant reduction of ctDNA in plasma could be detected (p = 0.98).
