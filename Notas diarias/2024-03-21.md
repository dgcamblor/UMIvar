# Notas

Although there has been an overall decline of 2% a year in colorectal cancer (CRC) mortality in the last decade, it still remains the second deadliest cancer across the globe (23). 

Nowadays, colorectal tumors are starting to be found in higher proportion in the left side of the colon or rectum (27% of the cases in 1995 and 31% in 2019), and also exhibit greater aggressiveness [[1]](https://www.zotero.org/google-docs/?eQam6q). Additionally, CRC is being diagnosed in younger people but not at an earlier stage; indeed, in patients who are less than 50 years old, incidence and mortality have increased by 1.5% and 2%, respectively [[1]](https://www.zotero.org/google-docs/?LtF5Wc). 

One of the main issues is that the gold standard techniques for screening and diagnosis of CRC are colonoscopy and tissue biopsy, which are invasive, bothering for the patient and can lead to complications [[2]](https://www.zotero.org/google-docs/?scLjDM).
