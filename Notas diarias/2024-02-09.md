## methylseq

`LEADING` and `TRAILING` options for Trimmomatic remove bases by their quality on the 5' and 3' ends, respectively.

```
LEADING:quality
```

`LEADING`: As long as base has quality value below `quality` threshold, the base is removed and the next base is investigated.

`fastp` implements a similar option:

> `fastp` supports per read sliding window cutting by evaluating the mean quality scores in the sliding window. [GitHub - OpenGene/fastp: An ultra-fast all-in-one FASTQ preprocessor (QC/adapters/trimming/filtering/splitting/merging...)](https://github.com/OpenGene/fastp)

- `-3, --cut_tail` move a sliding window from tail (3') to front, drop the bases in the window if its mean quality is below cut_mean_quality, stop otherwise.
- The window size is the number of nucleotides that will be evaluated at once. 
- If the window size is 1, this is similar as the Trimmomatic `TRAILING` method.

In both Trimmomatic and fastp, **Ns are removed**.

We can choose to use fastp: 

> Based on our evaluation, fastp is 2–5 times faster than other FASTQ preprocessing tools such as Trimmomatic or Cutadapt despite performing far more operations than similar tools.
