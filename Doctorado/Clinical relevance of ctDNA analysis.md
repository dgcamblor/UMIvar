The detection of ctDNA after treatment generally indicates poor patient prognosis.

## MRD detection

One specific area of interest is its role in assessing minimal residual disease (MRD) and the possibility of its use to guide therapeutic decisions. One hope is that it will be able to guide treatment in the controversial setting of adjuvant chemotherapy (ACT) in stage II and other non or oligometastatic colorectal cancers (CRC). The role of ACT in this setting is poorly defined because of the heterogeneity within disease stages [@chidharlaCirculatingTumorDNA2022]. Using ctDNA to guide the treatment can help avoid the toxic effects of chemotherapy after surgery, especially in patients with a low risk of recurrence.

In cancer types such as CRC, ctDNA can be used for [[Enfermedad mínima residual (EMR)|MRD]] assessment, being a strong prognostic marker of RFS [@chidharlaCirculatingTumorDNA2022]. ctDNA-based MRD detection can predict recurrence with lead times that precede standard imaging, with times up to 12 months [@kasiImpactCirculatingTumor2022].
