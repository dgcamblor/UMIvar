
## The problem

>Surgical resection of CRC liver metastases (CRLM) confined to the organ is a potentially curative therapy and offers superior long-term disease control compared to non-surgical therapies [47–49]. Perioperative chemotherapy prolongs disease-free survival, but the benefit on overall survival (OS) has never been demonstrated [50,51]. Therefore, there is no consensus on which mCRC patients should undergo postoperative systemic therapy. The identification of MRD after liver resection might help in the appropriate selection of patients for systemic therapy. [@jacomeMinimalResidualDisease2023].
