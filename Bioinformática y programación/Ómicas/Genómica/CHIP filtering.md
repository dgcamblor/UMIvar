## WBC filtering

The gold-standard of CHIP variant filtering is to sequence the white blood cell (WBC) fraction independently from the plasma.

## COSMIC annotation

Garcia et al. used COSMIC data to associate variants to CHIP status.

> While we do not have access to reference WBC sequencing information, we propose a practical methodology for correctly labeling CHIP variants in liquid biopsy data. Recently-published evidence showed that > 85% of CHIP variants have been observed in hematopoietic and lymphoid tissue samples[25](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-89592-8#ref-CR25 "Jaiswal, S. et al. Age-related clonal hematopoiesis associated with adverse outcomes. N. Engl. J. Med. 371(26), 2488–2498 (2014)."),[26](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-89592-8#ref-CR26 "Steensma, D. P. et al. Clonal hematopoiesis of indeterminate potential and its distinction from myelodysplastic syndromes. Blood 126(1), 9–16 (2015)."). We therefore classify any variant in the 0.1–20% AF range previously observed in hematopoietic and lymphoid tissue (COSMIC) as CHIP-associated. The prevalence of CHIP-derived variants is clearly identified in our data set and mirrors data found in WBC sequencing studies[25](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-89592-8#ref-CR25 "Jaiswal, S. et al. Age-related clonal hematopoiesis associated with adverse outcomes. N. Engl. J. Med. 371(26), 2488–2498 (2014)."),[26](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-89592-8#ref-CR26 "Steensma, D. P. et al. Clonal hematopoiesis of indeterminate potential and its distinction from myelodysplastic syndromes. Blood 126(1), 9–16 (2015).").
