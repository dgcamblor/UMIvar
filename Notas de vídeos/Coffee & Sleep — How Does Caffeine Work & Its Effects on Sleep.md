## AI summary

- [01:05](https://youtu.be/bFdBJ2v6sps?t=65s) 🧠 Caffeine, a psychoactive stimulant, increases dopamine and primarily acts on **adenosine receptors**, blocking their sleep-inducing effects. [02:02](https://youtu.be/bFdBJ2v6sps?t=122s) 🔄 Adenosine, accumulating in the brain throughout wakefulness, creates sleep pressure; caffeine competes with adenosine, blocking its effects temporarily.
- [05:45](https://youtu.be/bFdBJ2v6sps?t=345s) 🚨 **Caffeine crash** occurs when caffeine is metabolized; half-life of caffeine is around 5-6 hours, varying among individuals.
- [10:33](https://youtu.be/bFdBJ2v6sps?t=633s) 🕒 Recommended cutoff time for caffeine intake is about 8-10 hours before bedtime to avoid disrupting deep sleep; continuing caffeine into the evening can lead to dependency and decreased sleep quality.

## Notas

Caffeine works by competitively acting on adenosine receptors. It masks the sensing of adenosine. Caffeine is metabolized by CYP enzymes.
