## AI summary

- [16:28](https://youtu.be/hFL6qRIJZ_Y?t=988s) ⏰ People with ADHD often struggle with time perception, tending to run late and procrastinate, but they can focus well under high-stakes situations.
- [22:45](https://youtu.be/hFL6qRIJZ_Y?t=1365s) 🎯 People with ADHD can achieve heightened levels of focus, especially in activities that genuinely interest them, known as hyper-focus. It is not that people with ADHD can not focus in anything at all.
- [27:34](https://youtu.be/hFL6qRIJZ_Y?t=1654s) 🧊 Two main circuits relevant to ADHD: the default mode network (active during idle states) and task networks (goal-oriented); they normally function in an anti-correlated manner. [31:30](https://youtu.be/hFL6qRIJZ_Y?t=1890s) 🔄 In individuals with ADHD, the default mode network and task networks become correlated, working together in an abnormal way; treatment often restores the anti-correlation.
- [34:20](https://youtu.be/hFL6qRIJZ_Y?t=2060s) 🧪 Low dopamine hypothesis suggests that low dopamine levels lead to unnecessary firing of neurons, causing the default mode and task-related networks to be less coordinated. [37:47](https://youtu.be/hFL6qRIJZ_Y?t=2267s) 🌐 People with ADHD may engage in stimulant-seeking behaviors (e.g., caffeine, cigarettes, drugs) to self-medicate and increase dopamine levels for improved focus. [43:03](https://youtu.be/hFL6qRIJZ_Y?t=2583s) 🚨 Ritalin and Adderall, while structurally similar to street drugs, are prescribed stimulants that increase dopamine, norepinephrine, and adrenaline levels to enhance focus and energy.
- [01:26:46](https://youtu.be/hFL6qRIJZ_Y?t=9606s) ⏱️ Blinks, controlled by dopamine, play a role in time perception; understanding and controlling blinking can improve attention and time management, particularly in ADHD.
- [02:09:08](https://youtu.be/hFL6qRIJZ_Y?t=7748s) 📱 Excessive smartphone use can lead to attention deficits. Limiting usage to 60 minutes for adolescents and around 2 hours for adults may help maintain focus, according to a study.
